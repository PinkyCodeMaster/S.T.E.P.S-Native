## 5. Lightweight vs. Heavyweight Decisions

Lightweight items (UI spacing, copy tweaks, bug fixes) may compress the template but still require context, decision, and alignment. Heavyweight calls (monetisation, enforcement, behavioural nudges, data usage, recovery/risk flows) demand full detail across all template sections.

## 6. Reopening Decisions

Revisit only when new information appears, context changes materially, harm is observed, or legal/regulatory shifts demand it. Reopening requires a new record referencing the original and outlining what changed. History is extended, never erased.

## 7. Relationship to Other Documents

The log enforces the Ethical Product Charter, protects the roadmap, preserves intent behind the Feature Inventory, and supports governance. If a decision conflicts with another document, record the contradiction and mitigation.

## 8. Storage & Access

Keep the log versioned, searchable, and accessible to contributors‚Äînot hidden with leadership. Transparency builds internal trust.

## 9. Why This Matters

Products drift when memory fades, pressure mounts, and context disappears. The log slows us down just enough to stay honest and ethical.

## 10. Closing Statement

S.T.E.P.S relies on clarity, memory, accountability, and restraint. If a decision cannot be written calmly and ethically, it should not be made.
